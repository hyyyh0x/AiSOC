## The setup

Example Customer Inc. operates a regulated payments stack across two AWS
regions and a Microsoft 365 tenant. Before AiSOC, the SOC was running a
queue of roughly 5,000 alerts per day across two SIEM tools and a managed
EDR — most of them duplicates of the same underlying entity behaviour.

## What changed with AiSOC

The team enabled the connectors for AWS CloudTrail, M365 audit, and the
EDR. Risk-Based Alerting collapsed entity-level activity into a single
queue; the autonomous triage agent took the first pass on every promoted
alert; and the investigation ledger captured the prompt, tool calls, and
evidence for every decision.

Within a sprint:

- The daily review queue dropped from ~5,000 to ~320 entity-level alerts.
- Mean time to resolution fell from over four hours to twelve minutes for
  the bulk of routine cases (the L1 / L2 substitute path).
- 78% of triaged alerts auto-resolved with confidence-gated playbooks, with
  every action signed and replayable in the ledger.

## What the auditor saw

The SOC 2 Type II auditor opened the investigation ledger directly and
walked five sampled cases end-to-end: the prompts the agent issued, the
evidence rows it cited, and the actions it executed. No vendor summary.
That review closed three controls (CC7.2, CC7.3, CC7.4) in a single pass.

## What's next

Example Customer Inc. is rolling AiSOC out to its second business unit
and contributing two detection rules upstream — both expected to land in
the next community release.
