# AiSOC Trademark and Brand Policy

The AiSOC **code** is free under the [MIT License](LICENSE). The AiSOC **name and logo** are not part of that grant. This is deliberate: MIT lets anyone fork the code (that is the point), but a funded competitor should not be able to fork it, outspend the community on marketing, and pass their product off as "AiSOC."

## What you may do without permission

- Use, fork, modify, and redistribute the source under the MIT License.
- State truthfully that your product is "built on AiSOC," "powered by AiSOC," or "a fork of AiSOC," provided it is clear you are not the official project.
- Use the AiSOC name in blog posts, talks, comparisons, and documentation that refer to the project.

## What requires permission

- Naming a fork, product, service, or company "AiSOC" (or a confusingly similar name).
- Using the AiSOC name or logo as your own product/service branding, or in a way that implies official endorsement, affiliation, or that your distribution is the canonical one.
- Using the AiSOC logo in modified form, or on merchandise.

## Distributing a modified version

If you distribute a modified build, please rename it so users are not misled about what they are running and who supports it. You must keep the MIT `LICENSE` and copyright notices intact, but the product name should be yours.

## Hosted / managed offerings

Offering a hosted or managed service is permitted by the MIT License, but you may not market it under the AiSOC name or imply it is the official hosted AiSOC without permission.

## Requests

To request permission for a use not covered above, open a discussion or contact the maintainers via the channels in [`SUPPORT.md`](SUPPORT.md). We aim to be permissive for community, educational, and non-deceptive uses.

This policy may evolve as the project's governance matures (see [`ROADMAP.md`](ROADMAP.md) — governance phase). It does not restrict any right granted by the MIT License to the code itself.
